Pan-Seared Lamb Chops with Red Wine Sauce

Eight minutes in cast iron. A red wine reduction that tastes like you spent all day on it.

Prep Time 5 min
Cook Time 20 min
Total Time 25 min
Serves 2
Difficulty Easy

Ingredients

  • 4 Alder Creek loin chops (about 1 inch thick)
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (grapeseed or canola)
  • Salt & freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Red Wine Sauce
  • 1 shallot, finely minced
  • ½ cup dry red wine
  • ½ cup lamb or beef stock
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp cold butter (to finish)

Instructions

  1. 1

    Prep the chops

    Take the chops out of the fridge 20–30 minutes before cooking. Pat them completely dry with paper towels — this is the most important step for getting a proper sear. Season both sides generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  2. 2

    Get the pan screaming hot

    Place a cast iron skillet over high heat for 2–3 minutes until it begins to smoke lightly. Add the oil and swirl to coat. You want intense, dry heat — this is what creates the crust.

  3. 3

    Sear without touching

    Lay the chops in the pan and do not move them. Sear for 3–4 minutes per side — they'll release naturally when a crust has formed. In the final minute of the second side, add the smashed garlic, rosemary sprigs, and 1 tbsp of butter. Tilt the pan slightly and baste the chops continuously with the foaming butter.

  4. 4

    Rest the chops

    Transfer to a warm plate and tent loosely with foil. Rest for 5 minutes. This step is non-negotiable — the internal temperature will carry up slightly and the juices will redistribute throughout the meat.

  5. 5

    Build the pan sauce

    Pour off most of the fat from the pan, leaving about 1 tsp behind along with all the browned bits. Over medium heat, add the minced shallot and cook for 2 minutes, stirring, until softened and just starting to colour.

  6. 6

    Deglaze and reduce

    Pour in the red wine and scrape up every browned bit from the bottom — that's pure flavour. Let it reduce by half, about 2–3 minutes. Add the stock and Dijon mustard, stir to combine, and simmer until the sauce is glossy and lightly coats a spoon, about 3–4 more minutes.

  7. 7

    Finish and serve

    Remove the pan from the heat. Add the cold butter and swirl the pan — don't stir — until it melts into the sauce and gives it a glossy, velvety finish. Taste and adjust salt. Plate the chops and spoon the sauce over generously. Serve immediately.

Tips & Notes

  • Dry chops = better crust. Any moisture on the surface of the meat will steam instead of sear. Take the time to pat them thoroughly dry.
  • Don't crowd the pan. If the chops are touching, they'll steam each other. Use a large skillet or cook in two batches.
  • Internal temperature. For medium-rare, pull at 130–135°F (54–57°C). The carry-over during resting will bring it to a perfect rosy pink. Medium is 140°F (60°C).
  • Wine choice. Use a wine you'd drink — a Côtes du Rhône, Malbec, or Pinot Noir all work well. Avoid anything labelled "cooking wine."
  • Make it a weeknight dinner. The entire sauce takes under 10 minutes while the chops rest. Keep stock and a bottle of red on hand and this is a 25-minute meal any night of the week.
  • Pairs well with: crushed potatoes, wilted greens with garlic, roasted cherry tomatoes, or a simple bitter salad to cut through the richness of the sauce.
