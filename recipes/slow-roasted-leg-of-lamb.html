Slow-Roasted Leg of Lamb

Low and slow with garlic, lemon, and oregano. Tender enough to pull apart, flavourful enough to remember.

Prep Time 15 min
Cook Time 4 hrs
Total Time 4 hrs 15 min
Serves 6–8
Difficulty Easy

Ingredients

  • 1 bone-in leg of Alder Creek lamb (4–5 lbs)
  • 8 cloves garlic, sliced into thin slivers
  • 2 lemons, zested and juiced
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp dried oregano
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt & freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup chicken or lamb stock
  • 1 cup dry white wine (or extra stock)

Instructions

  1. 1

    Preheat the oven

    Set your oven to 325°F (165°C). Take the lamb out of the fridge 30–45 minutes before cooking so it comes to room temperature and roasts evenly.

  2. 2

    Stud with garlic

    Using a small sharp knife, make 20–25 deep slits all over the leg — top, bottom, and sides. Push a thin sliver of garlic deep into each one. This infuses the meat from the inside out as it roasts.

  3. 3

    Make the herb paste

    In a small bowl, mix the olive oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, dried oregano, rosemary, smoked paprika, and a generous amount of salt and pepper. It should form a loose paste. Rub it all over the lamb, pressing it into every surface and crevice.

  4. 4

    Set up the roasting pan

    Place the lamb in a deep roasting dish. Pour the stock and white wine around the base of the lamb — not over it. Cover the dish very tightly with foil. The steam is what makes it fall-apart tender.

  5. 5

    Low and slow

    Roast covered at 325°F for 3.5–4 hours. Check at the 3-hour mark — the meat should be very tender and starting to pull away from the bone. If it's resisting, give it another 30 minutes.

  6. 6

    Crisp the outside

    Remove the foil. Increase the oven temperature to 425°F (220°C) and roast uncovered for 20–25 minutes until the outside is deeply caramelized and golden.

  7. 7

    Rest and pull

    Transfer the lamb to a board and rest for at least 20 minutes. Use two forks to pull the meat apart — it should come apart effortlessly. Skim the fat from the pan juices and serve as a simple gravy alongside.

Tips & Notes

  • The foil seal matters. If steam escapes during the slow roast, the meat can dry out. Crimp the foil tightly around the rim of the dish.
  • Make it ahead. This dish reheats beautifully. Roast it the day before, refrigerate in its juices, and reheat covered at 325°F for 45 minutes. It may even be better the next day.
  • Bone-in vs. boned. Bone-in leg gives better flavour and keeps the meat moist. Boned and rolled (what we sell) works equally well and is easier to carve — just reduce cook time by 30–45 minutes and check internal temp at 190°F for pull-apart tenderness.
  • Pairs well with: roasted potatoes cooked in the same pan, Greek salad, warm flatbread, and a big dollop of tzatziki or yogurt.
  • Wine pairing: A Greek red like Agiorgitiko or a Grenache-based wine from the Southern Rhône are excellent matches.
