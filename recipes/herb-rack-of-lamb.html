Herb-Crusted Rack of Lamb

A golden crust of rosemary, thyme, and garlic pressed onto a perfectly seared rack. The showstopper of any table.

Prep Time 20 min
Cook Time 25 min
Total Time 45 min
Serves 4
Difficulty Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 1 rack of Alder Creek lamb (8 bones, french trimmed)
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup fresh breadcrumbs
  • ¼ cup flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt & freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. 1

    Rest the lamb

    Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Remove the rack from the fridge at least 30 minutes before cooking — bringing it to room temperature ensures even cooking throughout.

  2. 2

    Season generously

    Pat the rack dry with paper towels, then season all sides with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Don't be shy — lamb can handle it.

  3. 3

    Sear for colour

    Heat 1 tbsp of olive oil in an oven-safe skillet (cast iron is ideal) over high heat until shimmering. Place the rack meat-side down and sear for 3–4 minutes until deep golden brown. Briefly sear the fat cap side, then transfer to a board to rest for 5 minutes.

  4. 4

    Make the herb crust

    In a bowl, combine the breadcrumbs, parsley, rosemary, thyme, minced garlic, and remaining 2 tbsp of olive oil. Mix well and season with salt and pepper. The mixture should hold together when pressed.

  5. 5

    Apply the crust

    Brush the meat side of the rack with Dijon mustard — this acts as the glue. Press the herb crust firmly onto the mustard in an even layer, patting it down so it adheres well.

  6. 6

    Roast to medium-rare

    Return the rack to the skillet, crust-side up, or place on a roasting rack in a baking dish. Roast for 20–25 minutes until the crust is golden and an instant-read thermometer reads 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare.

  7. 7

    Rest, then carve

    Transfer to a cutting board and rest, uncovered, for 10 minutes. This step is non-negotiable — it allows the juices to redistribute. Carve by slicing between each bone to create individual chops. Serve immediately.

Tips & Notes

  • Temperature is everything. An instant-read thermometer is the best investment for cooking lamb. Pull it at 130°F for pink, juicy medium-rare.
  • French the bones. If you'd like the full presentation effect, wrap the exposed bones in foil before roasting so they stay clean white for serving.
  • Day-old bread makes better crumbs. Slightly stale bread crumbs hold their texture better under the oven heat than fresh.
  • Pairs well with: roasted root vegetables, a simple green salad, crushed potatoes with herb butter, and a mint yogurt sauce on the side.
  • Wine pairing: A Côtes du Rhône or a lighter Cabernet Franc are beautiful with the herby, garlicky crust.
