Our Story

A Family Farm, Five Generations Deep.

Alder Creek Farm has been part of Golden Lake, Ontario for five generations. What started as a piece of family land in the Ottawa Valley has grown into a thriving 80-ewe sheep operation — but the values have never changed.

We farm the way our family always has: let the animals graze freely, raise their young, and live the way nature intended. Our 200 acres of woodlots, pasture, and tillable fields give the flock plenty of room to roam.

We grow our own hay and silage — red clover, orchard grass, and timothy — right here on the property. We know exactly what our animals eat because we grow it ourselves. We believe in keeping it simple and doing it right.

We've also been proud participants in the Upper Ottawa Valley 4H Sheep Club, sharing our love of farming with the next generation and developing champion show animals along the way.

How We Farm

Raised on Grass. Finished with Grain.

We raise our lambs the way it should be done — on open pastures, with clean feed we grow ourselves, and the kind of care that only a family operation can give.

Rotational Grazing

Our flock grazes freely across our pastures every day. We rotate the land so the grass stays healthy and the animals always have fresh forage.

Our hay and silage — red clover, orchard grass, and timothy — is grown right here on the farm. No sourcing from unknown suppliers.
